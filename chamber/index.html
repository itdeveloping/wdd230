In 1919, before the culmination of the revolutionary movement, Ciudad Victoria experienced moments of political and social reorganization, its inhabitants, around 20 thousand, were willing to return to the path of development.

The government in power added elements to the urban structure and architecture of the capital, while the commerce sector, in those years, impatient but eager to strengthen itself, decided to group together to become a solid chamber organization with great expectations for the future.
